# Practitioners to Follow

The people shaping how AI-assisted coding is practiced, debated, and understood — from the book *Vibe Coding Bible, Vol 1: Creator's Playbook*.

---

## Visionaries & Founders

**Andrej Karpathy** — [@karpathy](https://x.com/karpathy) | [Blog](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/)
Former Tesla AI Director, OpenAI founding member, coined "vibe coding" in February 2025. His Software 3.0 thesis defines the intellectual horizon of the field. Key works: [The Vibe Coding Tweet](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1886192184808149383), [Vibe coding MenuGen](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/vibe-coding-menugen/), [2025 LLM Year in Review](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/year-in-review-2025/).

**Michael Truell** — [Cursor Blog](https://cursor.com/blog/series-d)
CEO of Anysphere. Built Cursor from a Stanford dorm project into a $29.3B AI-native IDE. His [Lex Fridman interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oFfVt3S51T4) is the definitive explanation of the "AI-native IDE" thesis. Key moment: [Series D announcement](https://cursor.com/blog/series-d).

**Amjad Masad** — [amasad.me](https://amasad.me/) | [TED Talk](https://www.ted.com/talks/amjad_masad_how_ai_can_help_you_turn_an_idea_into_the_next_great_app)
Replit CEO who bet the company on AI agents. His "agents all the way down" vision represents the most radical position on where software development is heading. Key works: [Agent v3 launch](https://blog.replit.com/introducing-agent-3-our-most-autonomous-agent-yet), [Effort-based pricing](https://blog.replit.com/effort-based-pricing).

**Guillermo Rauch** — [@rauchg](https://x.com/rauchg)
Vercel CEO, Next.js creator. His [v0.dev](https://v0.dev) tool turns natural language into React components, representing the design-to-code frontier. His thesis that "everyone's an engineer now" is either the future or the most dangerous idea in tech.

**Pieter Levels** — [@levelsio](https://x.com/levelsio)
Solo founder running $3M+/year businesses (NomadList, RemoteOK, PhotoAI) from a laptop. His [flight simulator built in hours](https://x.com/levelsio) via vibe coding demonstrates the maximum expression of the solo AI-augmented creator.

## Methodologists & Practitioners

**Simon Willison** — [simonwillison.net](https://simonwillison.net/tags/ai-assisted-programming/)
Django co-creator turned AI coding methodologist. The single best source for honest, nuanced reporting on what AI coding tools actually do and do not accomplish. Key works: [Not all AI-assisted programming is vibe coding](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Mar/19/vibe-coding/), [Here's how I use LLMs to help me write code](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Mar/11/using-llms-for-code/).

**Addy Osmani** — [addyosmani.com](https://addyosmani.com/blog/ai-coding-workflow/) | [Substack](https://addyo.substack.com/)
Google Cloud AI developer experience lead. His writings bring enterprise rigor to AI coding practices. Key works: [The 70% Problem](https://addyo.substack.com/p/the-70-problem-hard-truths-about), [Avoiding Skill Atrophy](https://addyo.substack.com/p/avoiding-skill-atrophy-in-the-age), [My LLM coding workflow going into 2026](https://addyosmani.com/blog/ai-coding-workflow/).

**Boris Cherny** — [borischerny.com](https://borischerny.com) | [@bcherny](https://x.com/bcherny)
Creator of Claude Code at Anthropic. His workflow posts reveal how the people building AI coding tools actually use them. Key work: [Claude Code: Best practices for agentic coding](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-best-practices).

**Paul Gauthier** — [Aider](https://aider.chat/) | [GitHub](https://github.com/paul-gauthier/aider)
Creator of Aider, the gold standard open-source terminal AI pair programmer. His benchmark-driven approach to tool development sets the standard for transparency in AI coding tool evaluation.

## Journalists & Analysts

**Gergely Orosz** — [The Pragmatic Engineer](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/software-engineering-with-llms-in-2025)
1M+ subscribers. His interviews with Kent Beck, Martin Fowler, and Simon Willison on AI coding are essential primary sources. Key works: [TDD, AI agents and coding with Kent Beck](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/tdd-ai-agents-and-coding-with-kent), [How Claude Code is built](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/how-claude-code-is-built).

**Shawn "swyx" Wang** — [swyx.io](https://www.swyx.io/) | [Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/)
Coined "AI Engineer" as a discipline. His Latent Space podcast connects the AI coding world to the broader AI ecosystem. The go-to source for understanding where AI engineering is heading.

**ThePrimeagen** — [GitHub](https://github.com/ThePrimeagen) | [Frontend Masters](https://frontendmasters.com/teachers/the-primeagen/)
The most influential AI coding skeptic on YouTube. Essential counterbalance to tool hype. His deep dives into performance, systems programming, and tool limitations provide critical perspective.

## Researchers & Academics

**Andrew Ng** — [@AndrewYNg](https://x.com/AndrewYNg)
Stanford professor, DeepLearning.AI founder. His [counterpoint to Karpathy](https://x.com/AndrewYNg/status/1904929635043074478) — that vibe coding "is a bad name for a very real and exhausting job" — captures the tension between accessibility and rigor.

**Kent Beck** — Creator of Extreme Programming and TDD
Invented the two practices most transformed by AI coding tools. His [2025 reflections on TDD with AI agents](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/tdd-ai-agents-and-coding-with-kent) are essential reading for understanding how foundational practices evolve.

**Martin Fowler** — [martinfowler.com](https://martinfowler.com/)
His [Legacy Modernization meets GenAI](https://martinfowler.com/articles/legacy-modernization-gen-ai.html) is a model of balanced, rigorous thinking about where AI genuinely helps and where the hype outpaces reality.
